# GPR132 interpolated-path results (real structures, both pH values)

Generated by [`examples/gpcr_pipeline_gpr132_string_demo.py`](../../gpcr_pipeline_gpr132_string_demo.py)
and [`examples/gpcr_pipeline_gpr132_string_ph6_demo.py`](../../gpcr_pipeline_gpr132_string_ph6_demo.py),
run on the real GPR132 (G2A) active/inactive homology models the user
provided. Same interpolated-path method as
[`examples/output/gpr68_string_demo/`](../gpr68_string_demo/README.md);
this file focuses on what's different for this receptor's input data and
how the results compare.

## Data-quality issue found and fixed before running anything

The raw active/inactive PDB files have exact atom correspondence
(380/380 residues, identical atom names) but a **misleading raw whole-chain
CA RMSD of 33.59 Angstrom** -- far too large to be a real conformational
difference (GPR68's was 9.42 A). Diagnosis, in order:

1. Per-residue displacement after a whole-structure Kabsch fit showed the
   N-terminus (residues 1-29, 10-17 A) and especially the C-terminus
   (356-380, 24-72 A, peaking at residue 373's 72 A) accounted for nearly
   all of it, while the ordered core (e.g. residues 127-166, 251-258,
   289-293) was already sub-3 A. Classic signature of an intrinsically
   disordered tail/terminus placed essentially arbitrarily by each
   independently-run AlphaFold-based model -- not a real difference.
   **Fix**: both structures trimmed to the well-ordered core, residues
   30-355 (`CORE_RESIDUE_RANGE`), before anything else. This needed a new
   shared helper, `examples/gpcr_pipeline_common.trim_structure_to_residue_range`.
2. Even after trimming, the raw core RMSD was still 18.99 A -- worse than
   GPR68's *entire receptor*. This time the cause was different: unlike
   GPR68 (whose two endpoint models were already co-registered in the same
   frame, needing no realignment), GPR132's active and inactive models are
   **not** in the same reference frame -- a real, unremoved rigid-body
   rotation/translation between the two files. After Kabsch-superposing
   the inactive structure onto the active structure's frame (fit computed
   on the core only, applied to every atom), the RMSD drops to a
   physically normal **2.60 Angstrom** -- in line with real GPCR
   activation-transition magnitudes and GPR68's own 2.6 A (after its own,
   simpler treatment). This is the number actually used for interpolation.

Both issues are checked and printed at the top of every run, not silently
assumed -- see `run.log`.

## Auto-detected motifs (and one honest limitation)

- DRY-motif Arg (TM3, 3.50): resnum 140 (GPR132's actual motif is **DRF**,
  not DRY -- required broadening `find_conserved_motifs`'s regex from
  literal DRY/ERY to the documented class-A variant set `[DE]R[YFWCH]`;
  verified this doesn't change GPR68's already-published result).
- TM7 P-x-x-Y motif Tyr (7.53): resnum 308.
- **Ionic-lock partner: not found (None)**, unlike GPR68 (which found
  resnum 224). Every Asp/Glu within reach of the DRY-Arg's guanidinium is
  within 15 residues of either the Arg or the Tyr motif position, so all
  are excluded by the same "don't pick up the motif's own D/N, or a
  residue that's probably just further along the same helix" rule that
  correctly finds GPR68's partner. This is the honest output of applying
  identical, unmodified criteria to a different receptor, not a bug --
  see the function's docstring for the regression check confirming this.
  Only `pxxy_dry_distance` and the RMSD-based path coordinates are
  available as geometric CVs for GPR132 as a result; `ionic_lock_like` is
  NaN throughout `path_table.csv`.

**Barrier height** (peak minus the lower endpoint, which for GPR132 is
always the active endpoint, unlike GPR68 where the trough was an
intermediate point): **347.0 kcal/mol at pH 7.4, 337.3 kcal/mol at pH
6.0 -- only a ~3% reduction.**

This is a genuinely different qualitative result from GPR68, worth stating
plainly:

- **GPR68**: the barrier *shape itself* changed with pH -- the pH-6.0
  curve, plotted relative to its own active endpoint, peaked at roughly
  half the height of pH-7.4's (`gpr68_string_demo/barrier_shape_relative.png`).
  Low pH preferentially stabilized the transition region over either
  endpoint.
- **GPR132**: `barrier_shape_relative.png` for this receptor shows the
  pH-7.4 and pH-6.0 curves nearly **overlapping** -- the barrier's shape
  and relative height are essentially pH-independent here, even though
  the receptor as a whole is real-and-substantially destabilized by low pH
  (+108 to +141 kcal/mol depending on position, a broadly comparable
  magnitude to GPR68's own +163 to +224 kcal/mol range). Also notice the
  shift is **not symmetric between endpoints** for GPR132: the inactive
  endpoint shifts more (+140.6) than the active endpoint (+118.4), a
  ~22 kcal/mol asymmetry not seen in GPR68's endpoint-vs-endpoint
  comparison (+217.5 vs. +223.8, a much smaller ~6 kcal/mol difference).

Two receptors, two different pH-response signatures, both computed with
identical methodology -- treat that contrast itself as the finding worth
following up on (e.g. does GPR132's flatter relative-barrier response
predict weaker pH-dependent conformational switching than GPR68's, given
both show comparable absolute destabilization?), not as evidence one
receptor's proton-sensing mechanism is "less real" than the other's --
the barrier heights here (300+ kcal/mol) are far larger than GPR68's
(~130-170 kcal/mol) in absolute terms, consistent with GPR132's core being
smaller (326 vs. 365 residues, fewer atoms to relax) but starting from a
larger raw conformational difference (2.6 A here vs. also 2.6 A for
GPR68 coincidentally, so barrier height differences aren't simply due to a
bigger structural change to interpolate across) -- this asks for further
investigation, not a conclusion drawn from N=1-per-point data.

## Known limitations

Same as `gpr68_string_demo`'s: not a converged string method (no
reparametrization or tangent-force projection -- barrier height/shape is a
first estimate), N=1 per (interpolation point, pH) with no error bars, and
geometric CVs (what's available, i.e. `pxxy_dry_distance`) are
auto-detected rather than GPCRdb-validated. Additionally specific to this
run: the core-trimming and rigid-body registration steps are themselves
judgment calls (a defensible, diagnosed, and disclosed one, but a
different receptor's disordered-region boundary might not fall as cleanly
at residues 30/355 as GPR132's did).
